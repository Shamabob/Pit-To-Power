Our Key Finding: Date Pit Oil Outperforms Kerosene

The Fossil Fuel Dilemma

Extensive reliance on crude oil leads to high greenhouse gas emissions like CO2 and the depletion of main fuel sources, contributing significantly to climate change.

Sustainable Alternative

We utilize date pits a major form of food waste in the UAE to extract a clean biofuel, solving both the energy crisis and the waste disposal problem.

Superior Efficiency

Calorimetry showed Date Pit Oil consistently produced a greater rise in water temperature than Kerosene, demonstrating higher energy efficiency per gram.

The Investigation Methodology

1. Obtaining Date Powder

  • Date pits collected from local mosques Ramadan waste.
  • Washed, dried 4-5 days, and roasted until charred dark brown.
  • Crushed using a mortar and pestle, then finely ground using a spice grinder and passed through a sieve.

2. Solvent Oil Extraction

  • Measured date powder added in excess to 200 ml of Ethanol.
  • Mixture filtered to obtain the oil/solvent solution filtrate.
  • Filtrate heated in a water bath at 78 °C to evaporate the ethanol and isolate the pure date pit oil.

3. Biofuel Efficiency Test

  • 100ml of Date Oil and 100ml of Kerosene added to separate spirit lamps.
  • Each lamp placed under a beaker containing 100ml of water, all starting at the same temperature.
  • Flames ignited simultaneously for 15 minutes.
  • Final water temperatures and total fuel mass consumed were recorded.
