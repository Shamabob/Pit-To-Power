Dual Project: Pit to Power & Phoenix Oasis

Our initiative began with the Pit to Power investigation, focusing on transforming date pits, a major waste product, into a high-efficiency alternative fuel.

Biofuel Focus & Findings

The Pit to Power project is a study in sustainable engineering, transforming agricultural waste into a viable energy source. Our process involves roasting and finely grinding the pits to maximize surface area and then isolating the oil by heating the ethanol solution in a water bath at 78°C to ensure complete solvent removal. This controlled methodology ensures high energy yield and reliability for comparative analysis.

Through solvent extraction and calorimetry testing, we proved that the Date Pit Oil extracted is an excellent energy source. Our comparative tests demonstrated that the biofuel consistently yielded a greater rise in water temperature than commercial Kerosene during controlled burns, validating its potential as a sustainable fuel for industrial applications.

Phase 2: Phoenix Oasis (Water Purification)

Building on the goal of utilizing waste for sustainable solutions, the Phoenix Oasis project became the second phase, focusing on the pits' powerful potential as a water purifying agent. This project explores the use of crushed date pits, a biological waste product, as a low-cost and eco-friendly solution for the removal of heavy metals from water, particularly benefiting rural and low income communities.

The Science of Adsorption & Health Impact

The aim is to investigate the effectiveness of date pit powder as a water purifying agent, to serve as a sustainable method of water purification. The core chemical efficacy stems from the pits' rich lignocellulosic materials (including cellulose, hemicellulose, and lignin), which contain functional groups such as hydroxyl, carboxyl, and phenolic groups. These groups facilitate the adsorption of heavy metal ions through mechanisms like ion exchange, complexation, and physical adsorption.

Adverse effects are present in organisms which over consume such metals: High levels of Aluminum can cause brain plaques and bone disease; Zinc can cause nausea and stomach cramps; Nickel can cause skin irritation and Manganese leads to memory problems. The project directly promotes safer, cleaner water access by targeting Nickel, Manganese, Iron, Aluminum, and Zinc removal.

Key Adsorption Results

The core finding is that the crushed date pit powder works as an effective adsorbent due to its rich lignocellulosic material. Experimental results showed a dramatic reduction in metal concentration in treated water, validating its capacity to remove heavy metals. For example, the detection time for Manganese in treated water increased from 264.0s to 666.0s, confirming extremely high adsorption efficiency.

Sustainability Commitment & SDGs

This combined project aligns with several UN Sustainable Development Goals, leveraging agricultural waste to create health and environmental value:

SDG 3 (Good Health): Reduces preventable diseases linked to long-term exposure to hazardous metals.
SDG 6 (Clean Water): Provides a natural, low-cost filtration method for communities with limited treatment resources.
SDG 7 (Affordable Energy): Explores renewable fuel (Pit to Power) and reduces energy needs for water treatment.
SDG 9 (Innovation): Pioneering novel, low-tech, and scalable solutions for resource management.
SDG 12 (Responsible Consumption): Turning agricultural waste (date pits) into valuable purification material.
SDG 13 (Climate Action): Reduces toxic emissions (Biofuel) and supports climate-resilient water solutions.
